§ 03 — The Five

Five things worth the time.

If only five things change on the site, these are the five. Three are genuinely critical, two are high-priority. None of them require a redesign.

01.
Critical · 2 hrs setup

Get the donate button working.

The "Help Elect Rickey" page repeats the phrase "Donation and sponsorship options coming soon!" several times, and the "DONATE" heading on the page doesn't actually link to a working donation flow which is a bit of a problem. I'd personally gate this off or give a real timeline of when the options should be available until it is working but thats just me. 

So basically anyone who shows up wanting to donate just can't. The campaign should get an Anedot or WinRed page set up (whichever Virginia rules allow) and link to it from the homepage and every page footer. Tell em to bring me my money...

02.
Critical · 4-6 hrs writing

Add a "what I'll do as sheriff" page.

Right now the site mostly says Rickey is experienced, ethical, fair, etc, which is all true and important. But there's nothing about what he'd actually do once in office. The whole site is a long résumé in 7 different fonts.

Voters wanna know the real stuff, like school safety, deputy retention, drug enforcement, mental-health response, that kind of thing. A Priorities page with 4-6 specific positions (maybe like 2-3 sentences each) would be the single biggest thing missing from the site. It doesn't need to be a novel, just give people something to point at.

03.
Critical · 15 min

Fix the phone number mismatch.

The contact page lists 804-396-0536. The downloadable resume PDF lists (804) 310-5452. Those are two different numbers, so one of them is wrong.

Pick whichever is the real campaign line and update everywhere, contact page, resume PDF, social profiles, all the same day. Voters who try to call the wrong one are going to be annoyed. Voters who try BOTH are going to be confused AND annoyed.

04.
High · 5 min

Fix the "Colonial Hines" typo.

On the Leadership & Values page, there's a photo caption that reads "Photo is the ceremony when Rickey was promoted to Major. Pictured are his son, daughter, and Colonial Hines."

The actual name is Colonel David R. Hines (per the Hanover County Sheriff's Office and the Virginia Sheriffs' Association). Misspelling a respected former sheriff's title in a photo caption on a website about wanting to be the next one is some Parker level bs to say the least.

05.
High · 1 hr

Build a voting info page.

There's no "Voting Information" page anywhere on the site. Should have the November 3, 2026 special-election date, the registration deadline, a polling-place link to elections.virginia.gov, and the absentee/early-voting info.

§ 05 — By Area

The rest of it, page by page.

Filter by severity to see what's worth tackling at each level. Critical first, then on down. you don't have to read the whole thing i'm not your professor.

Help Elect Rickey page

Critical
  • "Donation and sponsorship options coming soon!" appears repeatedly on the page
  • the "Donate" heading doesn't link to an actual donation flow
  • set up the donation processor first (Anedot is the standard for VA local races), then replace the "coming soon" message with a real working donate button

Visual design & consistency

High
  • the six "Synopsis" tiles on the homepage seem to use placeholder graphic GIFs instead of real photos
  • "Serving Hanover for more than 44 years..." appears as the heading on multiple unrelated tiles, looks like a copy-paste leftover
  • headings switch between sentence case, ALL CAPS, and Title Case all over the place. it's like four different people wrote this site and never spoke to each other. pick one style and apply it everywhere
  • basically only one real styled button on the entire site (the contact form's SEND). every other action is a tiny underlined "Click here to learn more…" link. the site is allergic to telling you what to do
  • the favicon is literally a file called Screenshot 2026-02-04 114431.png. somebody snapped it once on a Tuesday in February and it has lived there ever since without even working. should be a real designed favicon, even just "RD" on a navy background

Accessibility

High
  • the "Connect with Rickey" section has a blue Facebook link sitting on a red background. the letters look like they're bouncing. this is a real visual thing called chromatic aberration, not just preference, it physically hurts to look at. use white text on the red instead
  • some body text is mid-gray on white, hard for older voters to read. should be near-black
  • six identical "Click here to learn more…" links are bad for screen readers
  • verify every photo has real alt text in the GoDaddy editor

Homepage / hero

Medium
  • tells you who's running, but no buttons telling you what to do next. the hero needs 2-3 CTAs (Volunteer / Donate / Read Priorities)
  • the entire headline is baked into a single banner image instead of being real text. Google can't read it, screen readers can't read it, and the campaign can't update the wording without redoing the whole image
  • the headline within that image is stacked weird, "ELECT" / "Rickey R." / "DANDRIDGE" each on its own line, choppy. one tighter line would work better

Navigation

Medium
  • "Volunteer Sign-Up" is hidden under "More", should be top-level
  • "About Rickey" submenu has 8 items, that's overload, could be 2-3
  • no "Issues" or "Voting Info" page in the menu at all

Mobile experience

Medium
  • the volunteer form is loaded inside an iframe with its own scroll bar. on phones this creates a "scroll within scroll" feel that's frustrating
  • homepage hero stacks really tall, pushes "why vote for me" content way below the fold
  • the "Help Elect Rickey" tiles look tappable but do nothing, feels broken. tapping a thing and getting nothing back is the worst possible mobile experience, voters will close the tab

SEO & sharing

Medium
  • most page titles are bare ("Press Release", "Contact Us", "Education") with no campaign context. append "| Rickey R. Dandridge for Hanover County Sheriff" to every page title
  • add local keywords ("Mechanicsville", "Ashland", "Hanover Courthouse") to the homepage copy
  • Open Graph image is currently the banner with stacked text baked in. set og:image to a clean candidate photo
  • URL slugs are a mess — some -1 slugs are canonical, others are duplicates of real pages. needs a cleanup pass

Third-party widgets & trust

Medium
  • the "TrustedSite Certified" badge in the bottom-left of every page is loaded from a third-party script. it's a GoDaddy builder feature, not a campaign endorsement
  • "Powered by GoDaddy" in every single footer is genuinely embarrassing for a sheriff campaign. plan upgrade or migrate to remove. nobody trusts a candidate whose website is technically still a free trial
  • only Facebook is linked under social, add Instagram if it exists
  • no endorsements page, even 3-5 named supporters with photos would be a credibility boost

About / bio pages

Low
  • content is solid, 44 years, every rank, awards, community involvement. credible résumé
  • each subpage is short (50-150 words), feels thin, could consolidate into one richer About page with anchor links
  • some acronyms appear without being spelled out ("ASAP" and "WRVA" on the Awards page). worth a quick pass to spell them out the first time

Contact & performance

Low
  • strongest page on the site, real address, phone, email, working form
  • switch the campaign email from gmail to info@voterickeyrdandridgeforsheriff.vote, 5-min upgrade, looks more legit
  • add a subject-line dropdown to the form so the campaign can triage incoming messages
  • pages load reasonably fast on desktop. the hero banner is served larger than needed though, compress for mobile

§ 08 — Sources & Citations

Where the facts come from.

Every specific claim in the audit traces back to one of these. If anybody asks where i got it, here are the receipts. i didn't just make stuff up, i'm not a hater.

  1. Virginia Code § 24.2-956 — requirements for print and electronic campaign-ad disclaimers. The campaign's existing "PAID FOR AND AUTHORIZED BY RICKEY R. DANDRIDGE FOR SHERIFF" disclaimer is fully compliant, no treasurer name required for written or online materials.
  2. Hanover County Sheriff's Office — confirmed the correct title and name "Colonel David R. Hines" (the photo caption misspell on the Leadership & Values page).
  3. Virginia Sheriffs' Association — same name confirmation, with retirement announcement context.
  4. elections.virginia.gov — the official Virginia Department of Elections site, for polling-place lookup, registration deadline, and absentee/early-voting information that should be linked from the new Voting Info page.
  5. Hanover County Voter Registration: 804-365-6080 — for confirming the November 3, 2026 special-election date directly with the office before publishing it on the site.
  6. Anedot · WinRed · ActBlue — the standard donation processors for Virginia local campaigns. Pick one based on the campaign's party affiliation and treasurer's preference.
